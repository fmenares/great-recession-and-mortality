# great-recession-and-mortality

This project studies the effect of the **Great Recession (2008–2009)** on mortality in Mexico. The empirical strategy exploits cross-sectional variation in employment shocks across Mexican commuting zones (CZ), using economic census data from 2003, 2008, and 2013 to construct employment-to-population ratio shocks, and estimates their effect on age-adjusted mortality rates using two-way fixed effects (TWFE) regression.

## `codes/`

Contains all Stata (`.do`) scripts. Files prefixed with `00_` build the analytical dataset; files prefixed with `1_` run the analysis and produce outputs.

### `00_covariates.do`
Constructs the set of municipality-level covariates used in the analysis. Main steps:

1. **Municipality composition changes** – Imports INEGI records of municipal mergers (`M`) and dissolutions (`P`) between 2000 and 2015, so that only geographically stable municipalities are retained throughout the panel.
2. **Marginality Index** – Reads the CONAPO marginality index (1990–2015), merges with stable municipalities, and interpolates quinquennial values to produce an annual municipality-year panel of deprivation indicators (illiteracy, lack of services, overcrowding, income poverty, etc.).
3. **Social program beneficiaries** – Merges three transfer/insurance programs at the municipality-year level:
   - *Progresa/Oportunidades* (CCT, 1997–2015): beneficiary counts by locality-year from two administrative files (`fams_fase` and `newProg`), reshaped and collapsed to municipality level.
   - *Seguro Popular* (public health insurance, 2001–2015): municipality-year counts of affiliates.
   - *70 y Más* (unconditional cash transfer for elderly, 2007–2015): beneficiary counts by locality-year, collapsed to municipality level.
4. **Health infrastructure** – Processes Secretaría de Salud data (2001–2020) to produce municipality-year counts of hospitals, mobile clinics, health brigades, medical residents, doctors, and nurses.
5. **Population** – Imports CONAPO quinquennial population projections by municipality, sex, and 5-year age group (2000–2015), reshapes to a municipality-age-year panel, and saves. Also processes INEGI ITER census counts (2000, 2005, 2010) for locality-level population and insurance coverage.
6. **Household counts** – Interpolates household counts across census years for use as additional controls.

**Output:** Several intermediate `.dta` files saved to the `data/` directory (not tracked in this repo), including `mun_im_interpolated_2000_2015.dta`, `sp_pg_70_benef_mun.dta`, and `pop_5yr_muni.dta`.

### `00_death_data_INEGI.do`
Processes raw INEGI vital statistics death microdata (1998–2015) and constructs the main mortality dataset at the commuting-zone level. Main steps:

1. **Individual death records** – Iterates over yearly files (`defun98.dta` – `defun15.dta`), standardising variables: age at death, sex, marital status, nationality, ICD-10 cause of death, social security coverage (`derechohab`), and indicators for medical certification, death location, and Seguro Popular enrollment.
2. **ICD-10 cause classification** – Groups deaths into 10 cause categories: infectious diseases (A00–B99), neoplasms (C00–D48), circulatory diseases (I00–I99), mental disorders (F00–F99), endocrine/nutritional/metabolic (E00–E88), digestive diseases (K00–K93), perinatal conditions (P00–P96), homicides (X85–Y09), other external causes (V01–X84, Y10–Y89), and a residual "other" group.
3. **Heterogeneity indicators** – Generates death counts disaggregated by sex, marital status, insurance status (social security, Seguro Popular, uninsured), and cause × sex interactions.
4. **Collapse to municipality-age-year** – Collapses individual deaths to municipality × 5-year age group × year cells.
5. **Merge with population and economic data** – Merges with `pop_5yr_muni.dta` and the economic census CZ crosswalk (`econ_census_cz.dta`); drops municipalities with missing census observations.
6. **Collapse to CZ-year level** – Aggregates to commuting zone × year, computing:
   - Age-adjusted mortality rates (AAMR) using the year-2000 age structure as weights.
   - Age-specific death rates (ASDR) for five broad age groups (0–14, 15–49, 15–64, 50–69, 70+) by sex and cause.
   - Employment-to-population ratios and their pre–post differences (shocks) across census years (2003–2008, 2003–2013, 2008–2013).

**Output:** `mortality_shock_data.dta` – a CZ × year panel (1998–2015) containing all mortality and employment variables used in the regressions.

### `00_economic_census.do`
Processes the Mexican Economic Census (SAIC) to construct municipality-level employment variables and assigns commuting zones. Main steps:

1. **SAIC import** – Reads the 3-digit SCIAN industry aggregate from the SAIC export file (census years: 2002, 2007, 2012, 2017, 2022); keeps only the "Total municipal" row per municipality-year.
2. **Variable cleaning** – Renames and labels variables: number of firms, total employees (overall, male, female), hours worked, wage-earning employees (overall, male, female), total wages, severance payments, and bonuses.
3. **CZ assignment** – Merges with two commuting-zone crosswalks:
   - *Faber (2020)*: geo2_mx identifier crosswalk (~2,329 matched municipalities per census year).
   - *Banxico*: local labor market (`MERCADO_TRABAJO_LOCAL`) crosswalk.

**Output:** `econ_census_cz.dta` – municipality × census-year panel with employment variables and CZ identifiers.

### `1_analysis.do`
Produces descriptive figures showing mortality trends for the sample. Main steps:

- Loads the individual-level death panel (`deaths_02_15_did_panel.dta`), restricts to ages 60–79, and collapses to age group × locality × year cells.
- Merges with interpolated population denominators and computes crude and age-standardised mortality rates (AAMR) separately for the 60–69 and 70–79 age groups, by treatment status (covered vs. not covered by 70 y Más).
- Produces a time-series scatter plot (`death_rates_nc_2007_second_60_79.pdf`) with dual y-axes, vertical lines marking 2006 and 2007, and a legend distinguishing treatment group and age band.

**Output:** Figure saved to `$output/figures/raw_data/`.

---

### `1_models.do`
Estimates the main regression models and exports LaTeX tables. Main steps:

1. **Variable construction** – Loads `mortality_shock_data.dta` and creates log-mortality outcomes (`laamr`, `laamr_f`, `laamr_m`, cause-specific variants) and log age-specific death rates. Constructs "post-shock" interaction terms for each employment ratio shock × post-period (post 2007, 2008, or 2009).
2. **TWFE estimation** – Runs `reghdfe` with CZ and year fixed effects, population weights (`pop06`), and CZ-clustered standard errors, for each combination of:
   - Shock window: 2003–2008 or 2003–2013
   - Post-period cutoff: 2007, 2008, or 2009
   - Employment measure: total employment/pop, working-age employment/pop, wage-earning employment/pop, working-age wage-earning employment/pop
3. **Table export** – Writes regression coefficients, standard errors, mean dependent variable, number of observations, and number of CZs into LaTeX `tabular` environments.
4. **Event studies** – Runs `reghdfe` with continuous shock × year interactions (relative to a base year) for visual parallel-trends tests.

**Output:** LaTeX tables written to `$output/tables/`.

## `tables/`

Contains LaTeX tabular fragments (no `\documentclass` wrapper) ready to be `\input` into a paper.

| File | Description |
|------|-------------|
| `T1_03_08.tex` | 4-column table. Rows: Shock×Post 2007/2008/2009. Columns: four employment-to-population ratio measures using the **2003–2008** shock. Includes year and CZ FE, population weights, CZ-clustered SEs. |
| `T1_03_08_13.tex` | 8-column table extending `T1_03_08` to also include the **2003–2013** shock (columns 5–8), enabling comparison of short- vs. medium-run employment contractions. |

| Marginality Index | CONAPO | 1990–2015 |
| Progresa/Oportunidades beneficiaries | SEDESOL | 1997–2015 |
| Seguro Popular affiliates | CNPSS | 2001–2015 |
